# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Broad curious readers: a mix of technical and non-technical people who arrive via links, follow Marcus's thinking, or stumble in while searching. They read on laptop and mobile in a relaxed context. They care about ideas, craft, and personal voice — not just code. They are not necessarily the audience of a developer portfolio; they are the audience of a writer who also codes.

## Product Purpose

Marcus Wee's personal blog and project portfolio. A space to share writing, projects, and stray thinking. Success means a visitor feels they have stepped into a specific, real person's world, spent time reading something with substance, and wants to come back. The site is not a resume or a dashboard; it is a home.

## Brand Personality

Warm, considered, human. Like pulling a notebook off a shelf in a corner coffee shop: tactile, slightly worn, full of handwriting in the margins. Not a polished magazine, not a developer showcase. A place where someone actually thinks and writes.

## Anti-references

- Standard SaaS-minimalism (Vercel/Linear/Notion clones)
- Cold typographic editorial grids (Klim-influenced display serif + mono metadata + ruled separators)
- Generic developer blogs: white bg, Inter body, navy accent
- "Portfolio theater": curated cards, numbered case studies, metric callouts
- Anything that looks like it was assembled from a Tailwind template

## Design Principles

1. **Writing is the product.** The blog is the heart of the site. Typography, spacing, and layout must serve reading above all. No decoration competes with the text.
2. **Tactile over digital.** The interface should feel like something with physical weight — paper, ink, coffee-shop warmth — not glass, glow, or pixel-perfect finish.
3. **Asymmetric, not random.** Imperfect placement and varied rhythm signal human craft. But imperfection must be intentional: every rotation, margin shift, or offset serves the composition.
4. **Specific, not generic.** No design move should appear on any other developer blog. Every font, color, and accent element must be specific to this voice and these words.
5. **Restraint with texture.** Texture and decoration are welcome; clutter is not. Each ornamental detail earns its place or disappears.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum: 4.5:1 contrast ratio for body text, 3:1 for large text (≥18px or bold ≥14px). No content should be gated behind animation class triggers. Reduced-motion alternatives required for all transitions.
